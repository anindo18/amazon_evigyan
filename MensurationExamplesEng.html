Cylinder

Example 1 : The lateral surface area of a thin circular bottomed tin is 1760 sq. cm and radius is 10 cm. What is the height of the tin?

Given : The lateral surface area of a cylinder =2πrh = 1760 sq. cm.

radius = r = 10 cms

Solution : Lateral surface area of a cylinder = 2πrh

1760 =2*22/7*10*h

1760 =1760*7/2 x 22 x 10

h = 28 cms

Volume of Cylinder

Example 1 : Find the volume of the cylinder whose radius is 7 cm and height is 12 cm.

Given : Radius of the cylinder = r = 7 cm

Height of the cylinder = h = 12 cm

Solution : Volume of the cylinder V = Bh

= πr2h

=22/7x 7 x 7 x 12

22/7x 49 x 12

= 1848 cubic cms

∴ Volume of the cylinder = 1848 cc
