Quadratic Equations


An equation in which one or more of the terms is squared but raised to no higher power, having the general form ax2 + bx + c = 0, where a, b, and c are constants.

  1. An equation involved a variable is whose highest degree is one is a linear equation.

  2. A linear equation has only one root

  3. An equation involving a variable whose highest degree is two is a quadratic equation.

  4. Quadratic equation involving a variable only in second degree is a "Pure quadratic equation".
