Permutations and combinations

Permutations

Permutation is an orderly arrangement of objects.

The concept of permutations and combinations have applications in solving problems and genetic engineering.

In nPr, n and r are positive integers.n>=r

nPr means the number of permutations of 'n' things taking 'r' things at a time.

If one event occurs in 'm' different ways and another event occurs independently in 'n' different ways then the events together can be done in(m*n) different ways.

The product of first 'n' consecutive natural numbers is denoted by n! and is read as factorial n.

    Points to Remember

  1. Permutation is an orderly arrangement of objects.

  2. Fundamental Principle of Counting:If one event occurs in 'm' different ways and another event occurs independently in 'n' different ways,then the events together can be done in(m*n) different ways.

  3. nPr=n(n-1)(n-2)......(n-r+1)

  4. nPr=n!/(n-1)!

  5. nPn=n!

  6. 0!=1

  7. nPo=1

Combinations

Combinations is mere a selection of different objects.

In general the number of combinations of 'n' things taken 'r' at a time is denoted by nCr.

In nPr and nCr, both n and r are positive integers and n>=r.

If polygon has n sides, then it has (nC2=n) diagonals.

    Points to Remember

  1. Combinations is mere a selection of different objects.

  2. In general the number of combinations of 'n' things taken 'r' at a time is denoted by nCr.

  3. nCr=nPr/r!

  4. nCr=n!/(n-1)!r!

  5. nCn=1

  6. nCo=1

  7. nCn=nCn-r

  8. nC1=n

  9. nCr<=nPr

  10. If polygon has n sides, then it has (nC2=n) diagonals.
