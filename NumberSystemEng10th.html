Set Theory

Set theory is the branch of mathematics that studies sets, which are collections of objects.

Properties of set theory

  1. Commutative Property:

    1. Union of sets is commutative

      A ∪ B=B ∪ A

    2. Intersection of sets is commutative

      A ∩ B=B ∩ A

  2. Associative Property:

    1. Union of sets is associative

      A ∪ (B ∪ C)=(A ∪ B)∪ C

    2. Intersection of sets is associative

      A ∩ (B ∩ C)=(A ∩ B)∩ C

  3. Distributive Property:

    1. Union of sets is distributive over intersection of sets

      A ∪ (B ∩ C)=(A ∪ B)∩ (A ∪ C)

    2. Intersection of sets is distributive over union of sets

      A ∩ (B ∪ C)=(A ∩ B)∪(A ∩ C)

  4. De Morgan's laws:

    1. The complement of union of sets is the intersection of their complements

      (A ∪ B)'=A'∩ B'

    2. The complement of intersection of sets is the union of their complements

      (A ∩ B)'=A'∪ B'

  5. n(A ∪ B)=n(A)+n(B)-n(A ∩ B)

  6. n(A ∩ B)=n(A)+n(B)-n(A ∪ B)

  7. If A and B are disjoint sets,then A ∩ B=null

    1. n(A ∩ B)=0

    2. n(A ∪ B)=n(A)+n(B)

Sequence

  1. Definition of sequence:

    A sequence is an ordered arrangement of numbers according to a definite rule.

  2. The terms of sequence:

    The individual numbers that form a sequence are called terms of sequence.

    Example: The numbers 1,3,5,7,...... form a sequence 1,3,5,7,......are called the first,second,third,fourth......terms of sequences respectively.

  3. Finite and infinite sequence

    A sequence having a finite number of terms is a finite sequence.

    T1,T2,T3.....Tn is a finite sequence of n terms where Tn is a last term.

    A sequence having infinite number of terms is an infinite sequence.

    T1,T2,T3........ is an infinite sequence.

Arithmetic Progression

  1. Definition of Arithmetic Progression:

    An arithmetic progression is sequence in which the difference between a term and it's preceding term is a constant.The constant is the common difference (c.d) and is denoted by 'd'.

    The arithmetic progression is abbreviated as A.P.

  2. The general form and term of an A.P:

    Tn=a+(n-1)d is the general term of an A.P.

    In an A.P. succeeding term of a given term is obtained by adding 'd' to it.[Tn+1=Tn+d].

    In an A.P. preceding term of a given term is obtained by subtracting 'd' to it.[Tn-1=Tn-d].

    3. Points to Remember:

    1. An Arithmetic progression is a sequence in which the difference between a term and its preceding term is constant.

    2. The difference between a term and its preceding term in an A.P is called the common difference and denoted by 'd'

    3. The general form of A.P.is a,a+d,a+2d,a+3d,.........a+(n-1)d.

    4. A series form of A.P is an arithmetic series.

    5. The sum of the first 'n' natural numbers is Sn=n(n+1)/2.

    6. The sum of first 'n' terms of an A.P if the last term is not given.Sn=n/2[2a+(n-1)d]

    7. The sum of first 'n' terms of an A.P.if the last term is given.Sn=n/2[a+l]

    Geometric Progression

    1. The definition of a Geometric Progression:

      A Geometric progression is a sequence in which the ratio of term and it's preceding term is constant.This constant is called the common ratio and is denoted by "r"

      Geometric Progression is abbreviated as G.P.

    2. The general form and general term of a geometric progression

      Tn=arn-1 is general term of the G.P,where 'a' is first term and 'r' is common ratio.

      To obtain the succeeding term of a given term Tn multiply it by r i.e Tn+1=Tn*r

      To obtain the preceding term of a given term Tn divide it by r i.e Tn+1=Tn/r

    3. Points to Remember:

      1. A geometric progression is sequence in which the ratio of a term and its preceding term is constant.

      2. In a G.P. the ratio of a term and its preceding term is called common ratio(r).

      3. The general form of G.P. is a,ar,ar2,..........arn-1

      4. The nth term of a G.P.is Tn=arn-1

    Harmonic Progression

    1. The definition of Harmonic Progression

      A Harmonic progression is a sequence in which the reciprocal of terms of it form an arithmetic progression.

    2. The general form of a Harmonic Progression

      Tn=1/a+(n-1)d.

      The reciprocal terms of a H.P form A.P.

      There is no formula to find the sum of n terms of a H.P as in the case of A.P. and G.P.

    3. Points to remember:

      1. A Harmonic progression is a sequence in which the reciprocal of terms of it form an arithmetic progression.

      2. The general form of a Harmonic progression is:1/a,1/a+d,1/a+2d,.......1/a+(n-1)d

      3. The nth term of H.P is:Tn=1/a+(n-1)d

    Matrices

    1. The definition of Matrices

      a matrix is a arrangement of numbers in terms of rows and columns enclosed within brackets

      The horizontal arrangements are called 'Rows' and vertical arrangements are called 'Columns'.

    2. Multiplication of a matrix by a scalar

      When a matrix is multiplied by scalar K then all its elements have to be multiplied by K.

    3. Transpose of a matrix

      Transpose of a matrix is the matrix obtained by changing its rows in to corresponding columns or columns in to corresponding rows.

      (A')=A

      If A=A' then matrix A is a symmetric matrix

      If A = -A' then the matrix A is a skew symmetric matrix.

      If two matrix are equal then their corresponding elements are equal.

    4. Addition of Matrices

      If A and B are two matrices of same order then the matrix obtained by adding the corresponding elements of A and B is sum of matrices A and B it is denoted by (A+B).

      To add two matrices,their orders must be same.

  4. Subtraction of Matrices

    To subtract two matrices,their orders must be same.

    If A and B are two matrices of same order then their difference (A-B) is the matrix obtained by subtracting the corresponding elements of matrix B from the elements of matrix A.

    A+A' is a symmetric matrix.

    A-A' is a skew symmetric matrix.

  5. Multiplication of matrices

    If the number of columns of matrix A and the number of rows of matrix B are equal then only AB exists.

    If A is matrix of order (m*n) and B is order (n*p) then AB exits and its order is (m*p).

    If A and b are two matrices conformable for multiplication then (AB)'=B'A'

    If A is a square matrix and I is unit matrix of order A then AI=IA=A.
