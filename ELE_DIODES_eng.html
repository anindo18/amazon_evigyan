DIODES


You have learnt about the n-type and p-type semiconductors in the previous section. When a donor impurity is added to one side and an acceptor impurity is added to the other side of a pure semi conductor, the first side becomes n-type and the other side becomes p-type. Thus a p-n junction is formed (Fig 3.2) This is called a junction diode or a semiconductor diode.

Forward and Reverse Biasing : When an external source of e.m.f (usually of low value) is connected to a p-n junction, it is said to be biased and the applied voltage is called bias voltage.

When the positive terminal of a cell (source of e.m.f) is connected to p-side and the negative terminal to the n-side of a p-n junction, it is said to be forward biased

When the positive terminal of a cell is connected to the n side and the negative terminal to the p side of a p-n junction it is said to be reverse biased.

In this case the charge carriers are repelled away from the junction and no current flows through the junction. The device offers high resistance.

Thus p-n junction allows current to pass through, preferentially in one direction only; this action is called rectifying action and the device is called ‘rectifier’.

Applications : A diode has a number of applications in view of its rectifying action and current detecting action
(i) Diodes are used to convert A C into D C
(ii) They are used in voltage regulation systems
