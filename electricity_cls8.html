DEFINITION:

Electricity is the set of physical phenomena associated with the presence and flow of electric charge. Electricity gives a wide variety of well-known effects, such as lightning, static electricity, electromagnetic induction and the flow of electrical current. In addition, electricity permits the creation and reception of electromagnetic radiation such as radio waves.

UNIT OF ELECTRICITY:

The unit of measurement of electricity is "Joule"

Since, joule is a very small unit, a small unit called Kilojoule is also used.

1KJ = 1000J


ELECTRIC POWER:

The rate at which work is done by electricity is called electric power.

Electric Power= The work done by electricity / time

Practical unit of Electricity

For practical use, the unit for electricity used is kiloWatt-hour(kWh)
